Travelling overseas Travelling checklist

  • Travelling checklist

    Before you get packing, tick off these travelling essentials:
    Check your passport is valid

    Make sure your passport is valid for at least 6 months from your departure date but check the requirements of the countries you intend to visit to see if that is sufficient.

    Check you have a valid visa

    Depending on the country which you plan to enter and the reason for your travel there may be a requirement to obtain an appropriate visa. Visit the relevant consulate website or contact your travel agent for further information.

    Copy your travel documents

    Scan a copy of your passport, tickets, credit cards, itinerary, insurance documents and keep them in a safe place.

    List emergency contacts

    Keep a list of handy numbers, including your bank's lost or stolen cards number (call Westpac when you're overseas, +61 3 6345 1058. Reverse charge is available for lost or stolen cards).

    Contact your bank

    Call your bank to let them know you'll be overseas so they don't suspect fraud and cancel your credit cards.

    Register your travel plans

    Go to the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade site and register to be contacted in an emergency.

    Organise travel insurance

    Choose the right amount of cover for your needs and take photos of your valuables in case you need to claim. Visit our full website to view our travel insurance products.

    Order foreign currency

    Get your currency/travellers cheques; or get a travel money card with local currency to lock in the exchange rate.

    Use our Global ATM Alliance partners

    Save on fees by using our global alliance ATMs while you're overseas.

    Accessing savings or transaction accounts overseas

    ATM systems vary between countries so make sure you have a 4-digit PIN for each card including credit cards & your accounts are linked correctly to the cards you intend using whilst overseas. Some foreign country ATMs do not allow access to accounts linked to credit cards, you may need to order a Handycard before your leave.

    Check-in for your flight before you leave home

    Save time by checking in online and reserving seats.

    Turn off data roaming

    International data roaming costs can be steep so make sure you turn it off. You can still do your banking using our Mobile Banking app through Wi-Fi1.
