Buying a home What I need to know

  • What should I do first?

    The first steps to getting a loan.
    • Understand the real cost of buying a home. It’s more than just the price you’ll pay for a home, there are other expenses, including:

      ▪ Government stamp duty
      ▪ Solicitor/conveyancer fees
      ▪ Building and pest inspection fees
      ▪ Home and contents insurance
      ▪ Renovating or build costs.

    • Know your price limit. Use our calculators to get an idea of what you could borrow and what your repayments might be:
      ▪ How much can I borrow?
      ▪ What will my repayments be?

    • Talk to a Home Loan Expert. They’ll tell you exactly what you can borrow, based on your deposit, income and your other personal information. You’ll then know how much you can offer for your new home.

  • What do we offer?

    A home loan to suit you.

    Whether you’re buying a home or just refinancing, we have a range of home loan options to suit almost anyone. On all or some of our loans we offer:

    • Loans with a fixed or variable interest rate or a combination of both
    • Your choice of weekly, fortnightly or monthly repayments
    • Depending on the size of your loan, you could save thousands of dollars when you bundle it with our home loan package
    • 100% mortgage offset account
    • Competitive interest rates
    • Over 500 home loan experts around the country who’ll guide you through every step of the home loan journey.

  • How to save?

    Save on your home loan and a range of products.

    You could save thousands of dollars a year by bundling your loan with our home loan package – excludes our Flexi First Loans.

    • Save with an interest rate discount of up to 0.7% p.a
    • Save $696 - we remove the loan establishment and maintenance fees
    • Save with discounts on a range of other Westpac products.

  • How to apply?

    Information you’ll need to know before you apply.

    All our home loan applications are completed with the help of one of our home loan experts either on the phone or at a branch.

    Information you will need to provide when you apply:

    • Details of monthly expenses, including loans and credit card debt
    • List of assets (what you own) and liabilities (what you owe)
    • Confirmation of income, including trusts and investments
    • Latest super statement
    • Proof of savings e.g. statements
    • Contract of sale for the property you plan to buy, if available
    • Proof of identity for new customers, e.g. driver’s licence or Medicare card.

    If you’re a PAYG employee you’ll also need:

    • 2 recent pay slips showing monthly salary
    • Most recent payment summary or tax return.
       

    If you’re self-employed you’ll also need:

    • Business/company tax returns for last 2 years
    • Personal tax returns and assessment notices for last 2 years.
       

    If you’re refinancing you’ll also need:

    • Loan statements for last 6 months
    • Land title description (if known).
       

    If you’re building a home you’ll also need:

    • Plans, specifications, council approval and builder’s contract
    • Owner Builder Independent Adviser’s Report (if you’re the builder).

  • What happens after I apply?

    The steps after you’ve submitted your home loan application.
    • We value the property to ensure it meets your loan requirements
    • Finalise the processing of your loan - now the property valuation is done
    • Approve your loan - if your application meets our normal lending criteria
    • Your Home Finance Manager walks you through your loan contract
    • You take the loan contract to your solicitor or conveyancer for an independent review
    • You sign and return your loan documents to your Home Finance Manager
    • Settlement - ownership of the property passes from the current owner to you
    • Move in.
